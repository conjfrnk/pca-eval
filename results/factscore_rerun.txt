============================================================
KEY FINDINGS
============================================================
1. Evidence decomposition is CRITICAL: +20pp accuracy over truncated evidence.
   FActScore Wikipedia articles are long; naively truncating to 5000 chars
   loses the relevant evidence for most facts.

2. BM25 relevance ranking works well: The relevant_sentences mode uses
   query-aware sentence selection to find the most informative evidence
   chunks, providing a 5x NLI call overhead but dramatically better recall.

3. Model size helps WITH proper evidence: Large model gains +4pp over base
   when evidence is decomposed, but is actually WORSE with truncated evidence.

4. Gap to previous best: The 72% pre-trained result vs 83.8% XGBoost
   confirms that fine-tuning + multi-signal ensemble provides an additional
   ~12pp, which is consistent with other benchmarks (HAGRID: +20pp from
   XGBoost, AttributionBench: +4pp).

5. NOT_SUPPORTED is easy: All configurations achieve 90%+ on NS. The
   challenge is correctly identifying SUPPORTED facts, where evidence
   quality determines success.
