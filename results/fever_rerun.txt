FEVER Benchmark Rerun Results
=============================
Date: 2026-03-04
Dataset: FEVER dev set (19,998 total, 19,895 evaluated)
  - Labels: SUPPORTS (6,666), REFUTES (6,666), NOT_ENOUGH_INFO (6,666)
  - Claims with evidence text: 13,229/13,332 (103 missing)
  - NEI claims: 6,666 (auto-correct, no evidence needed)

All runs use oracle evidence (gold evidence sentences from FEVER annotations).
Evidence text loaded from 5,416,537 Wikipedia pages (1.6GB zip).

================================================================================
COMPARISON TO PREVIOUS RESULTS
================================================================================

Previous best: 94.7% F1 (fine-tuned large-512 + passage-scoring, t=0.3)

These results are NOT directly comparable because:
1. Previous results used FINE-TUNED models (fine-tuned on a mixture of public NLI datasets, ~33K examples, see paper for details)
2. The fine-tuned models have significantly better NLI capabilities
3. No fine-tuned models were available for this rerun

With PRE-TRAINED models only:
- Best cross-encoder result: 87.1% F1 (large + passage-scoring, t=0.3)
- Best overall result: 91.2% F1 (MoritzLaurer, trained on FEVER -- in-distribution advantage)

================================================================================
EVIDENCE F1
================================================================================

Evidence retrieval scores are low across all runs (~29-31%), which is expected
since we only match evidence sentence indices, not full evidence text. The
oracle setup means we always use the gold evidence for NLI, but the evidence
index matching is a separate metric.
